About Me

Hi, welcome to my homepage! I am Behzad Tahmasebzadeh, a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Michigan. Currently, I am working on the dynamical modeling of different types of galaxies. I was born in the beautiful city of Esfahan in Iran. Since high school, I fell in love with the night sky and desired to study astronomy. I got my bachelor's degree in physics at the University of Bu-Ali Sina (2013). My master's degree is in Astrophysics from the Institute of Advanced Studies in Basic Science (2016), a forerunner basic science institute in Iran. I worked on inflationary cosmological models in the scalar-tensor gravity framework. Later, my research interests revolved around the field of galaxy structure and evolution. I moved to China in 2017 to the wonderful mega-city of Shanghai and got my PhD degree at the Shanghai Astronomical Observatory (2021). My PhD thesis aimed to develop the methods and codes for dynamical modeling of barred galaxies, that was a joint project with Max Planck Institute for Extraterrestrial Physics in Germany.

Research

Research interest:

Research Projects:

Galactic dynamics:
- Developing methods and code for dynamical modeling of barred galaxies.
- Study the X-shaped orbital structures in B/P bulges using N-body simulations.
- Applying modified Schwarzschild code to real barred galaxies from IFU surveys.
- Measuring black hole mass in ultra dwarf galaxies in Virgo cluster using cycle1 JWST data

Cosmology:
- Study inflationary models in Brans-Dicke gravity using Planck data.
